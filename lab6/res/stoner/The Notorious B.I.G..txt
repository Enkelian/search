The Notorious B.I.G.
Christopher George Latore Wallace (May 21, 1972 – March 9, 1997), widely known by his stage name The Notorious B.I.G., or, more commonly among the familiar, Biggie Smalls or simply Biggie, was an American rapper, in the New York and gangsta rap traditions, who ranks among the greatest rappers ever. With a lyrical delivery laidback and yet slick, belying the lyrics' often grim content and his own intimidating appearance, his songs were often semi-autobiographical, telling of hardship and criminality, but also of debauchery and celebration.Born and raised in Brooklyn, New York City, Wallace signed to Sean "Puffy" Combs' label Bad Boy Records as it launched in 1993, and gained exposure through features on several, other artists' singles that year. His debut album, Ready to Die, released in 1994, now his magnum opus, made him the central figure in East Coast hip hop, and restored New York's visibility at a time when gangster rapper Snoop Dogg, of the Los Angeles rap scene, was dominating the rap genre. The following year, Biggie led his protégé group Junior M.A.F.I.A., a team of himself and longtime friends, including Lil' Kim, to chart success.

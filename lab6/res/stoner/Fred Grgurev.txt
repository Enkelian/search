Fred Grgurev
Ferdo "Fred" Grgurev (born September 14, 1951 in Zadar, Croatia in what was then Yugoslavia) is a retired Yugoslavian-American soccer forward who played professionally in the North American Soccer League and Major Indoor Soccer League. He also earned 14 caps, scoring one goal, for the United States men's national soccer team. He is now the owner of the famed New York City steakhouse, Delmonico's. Professional In 1976 and 1977, Grgurev played for the New York Apollo in the American Soccer League. In 1978, he joined the New York Cosmos of the North American Soccer League. In the fall of 1978, he began his indoor career with the Philadelphia Fever of the Major Indoor Soccer League. He led the league in scoring and was a First Team All Star. He was back in the NASL in 1979, this time with the Rochester Lancers. He went on to play for the Fever again during the 1979-1980 indoor season and the Lancers during the 1980 outdoor season.

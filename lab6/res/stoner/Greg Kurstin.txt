Greg Kurstin
Gregory Allen Kurstin (born May 14, 1969) is an American record producer, multi-instrumentalist and songwriter. He has been associated with releases which have cumulatively sold more than 85 million albums worldwide. He has won seven Grammy Awards, including Producer of the Year (Non-Classical) in 2017 and 2018.Kurstin co-wrote, produced and played most of the instruments on the record-breaking 2015 Adele single "Hello". Among others, he has worked with Sia, Beck, Kelly Clarkson, Paul McCartney, Pink, Zayn Malik, Lily Allen, and the Foo Fighters. He often plays guitar, bass, keyboards and drums, and engineers and programs the records he produces.Kurstin began his career as a jazz pianist and later co-founded Geggy Tah. He has collaborated with Inara George in The Bird and the Bee since 2004. Early life and education Kurstin was born and raised in Los Angeles, California. He started playing piano at age 5. Soon after, he picked up guitar and bass. Kurstin joined his first band at the age of 11, and co-wrote "Crunchy Water", the B-side to classmate Dweezil Zappa's "My Mother Is a Space Cadet" at 12.In high school, Kurstin focused on jazz piano.

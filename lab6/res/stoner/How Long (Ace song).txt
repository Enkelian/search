How Long (Ace song)
"How Long" is a 1975 song by the British group Ace from their album Five-A-Side. It reached No. 3 in the US and Canadian charts, and No. 20 in the UK Singles Chart.Although widely interpreted as being about adultery, the song was in fact composed by lead singer Paul Carrack upon discovering that bassist Terry Comer had been secretly working with the Sutherland Brothers and Quiver. Comer returned to Ace in time to play on the song.The guitar solo is by lead guitarist Phil Harris. Alan 'Bam' King was the band's rhythm guitarist, formerly with the Action. Chart history Weekly charts Year-end charts Covers Charting versions Lipps Inc. recorded a cover in 1980 that reached No. 4 on the U.S. dance chart, No. 29 on the U.S. soul singles chart, and No. 42 in Canada. Rod Stewart in 1981 recorded a version that charted in the top 50 of the Billboard Hot 100. It was included on his album Tonight I'm Yours. In 1993, British reggae group Aswad and singer Yazz recorded a cover of the song which reached No. 31 in the UK Singles Chart, from Yazz's 1994 album One on One.

2004 Nobel Peace Prize
The 2004 Nobel Peace Prize was awarded to Wangari Maathai "for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace". Overview |[Wangari Maathai, founder of the Green Belt Movement and known for her social, environmental, and political activism, was awarded the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize for her "contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace". She had received a call from Ole Danbolt Mjos, chair of the Norwegian Nobel Committee, on 8 October informing her of the news. She became the first African woman, and the first environmentalist, to win the prize. Having earned her Ph.D. in 1971, she is the first woman to earn a doctorate degree in East and Central Africa. == References ==

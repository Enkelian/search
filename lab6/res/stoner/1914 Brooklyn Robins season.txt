1914 Brooklyn Robins season
With Wilbert Robinson taking over as the new manager, many in the press began using the nickname Brooklyn Robins for the 1914 season along with other names. The Robins finished in 5th place, just missing finishing with a .500 record. Offseason December 20, 1913: Dick Egan was purchased by the Robins from the Cincinnati Reds. Regular season Season standings Record vs. opponents Notable transactions June 27, 1914: Joe Riggert was purchased from the Robins by the St. Louis Cardinals. July 6, 1914: Casey Hageman was purchased from the Robins by the Chicago Cubs. August 7, 1914: Bill Steele was purchased by the Robins from the St. Louis Cardinals. August 10, 1914: Red Smith was purchased from the Robins by the Boston Braves.

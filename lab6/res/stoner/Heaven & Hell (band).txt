Heaven & Hell (band)
Heaven & Hell were an English-American heavy metal band active from 2006 to 2010. Heaven & Hell was a collaboration between Black Sabbath founding members Tony Iommi and Geezer Butler and former Black Sabbath and Dio members Ronnie James Dio and Vinny Appice. The band's name was taken from the title of the first Black Sabbath album to feature Dio after original vocalist Ozzy Osbourne was fired in 1979. The four members of Heaven & Hell recorded and toured together as Black Sabbath from 1980 to 1982, and again from 1991 to 1992.

Eurasian collared dove
The Eurasian collared dove (Streptopelia decaocto) is a dove species native to Europe and Asia, which has been introduced to North America. Because of its vast global range and increasing population trend, it has been listed as Least Concern on the IUCN Red List. Taxonomy The Eurasian collared dove was formally described in 1838 by the Hungarian naturalist Imre Frivaldszky under the binomial name Columba decaocto. The specific epithet is Latin for "eighteen". The number comes from a Greek myth. A maid was unhappy that she was only paid 18 pieces a year and begged the gods to let the world know how little she was rewarded by her mistress. Thereupon Zeus created this dove that has called out "Deca-octo" ever since. The type locality is Plovdiv in Bulgaria. The Eurasian collared dove is now placed in genus Streptopelia that was introduced in 1855 by the French ornithologist Charles Lucien Bonaparte.

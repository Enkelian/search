1945 Brooklyn Dodgers season
As World War II was drawing to a close, the 1945 Brooklyn Dodgers finished 11 games back in third place in the National League race. Offseason February 24, 1945: Carden Gillenwater was purchased from the Dodgers by the Boston Braves. March 28, 1945: Whit Wyatt was purchased from the Dodgers by the Philadelphia Phillies. Regular season Eddie Stanky led the NL in runs scored with 128 in 1945, when he drew a then-record 148 walks.

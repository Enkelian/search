Robin Day
Sir Robin Day (24 October 1923 â€“ 6 August 2000) was an English political journalist and television and radio broadcaster.Day's obituary in The Guardian by Dick Taverne stated that he was "the most outstanding television journalist of his generation. He transformed the television interview, changed the relationship between politicians and television, and strove to assert balance and rationality into the medium's treatment of current affairs". Early life Day was born in Hampstead Garden Suburb, London, the youngest of four children, of a Post Office telephone engineer who became a GPO administrative manager. He received his early formal education at Brentwood School from 1934 to 1938, briefly attended the Crypt School, Gloucester, and later Bembridge School on the Isle of Wight. During World War 2 he received a commission into the British Army's Royal Artillery, with which he served from 1943, being deployed to East Africa, and thereby seeing little action. He was discharged from the British Army in 1947 with the rank of Lieutenant, and went up to St Edmund Hall, Oxford to read law.

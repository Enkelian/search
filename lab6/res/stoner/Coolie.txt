Coolie
The word coolie, meaning a labourer, has a variety of other implications and is sometimes regarded as offensive or a pejorative, depending upon the historical and geographical context, however in the origin country of India it is still considered a derogatory slur. It is similar, in many respects, to the Spanish term peón, although both terms are used in some countries, with slightly differing implications. The word originated in South Asia in the 17th century and meant day labourer, but since the 20th century the word has been used in that region to refer to porters at railway stations. During the 19th and early 20th centuries, the term coolie was most often used regarding indentured labourers originating from South Asia, South East Asia or China. Coolie is now regarded as derogatory and/or a racial slur in the Caribbean, Africa, Oceania, North America, South America, Southeast Asia and Europe – in reference to people from Asia. This is particularly so in South Africa, East Africa, Trinidad and Tobago, Guyana, Suriname, Jamaica, Mauritius, Fiji, and the Malay Peninsula.

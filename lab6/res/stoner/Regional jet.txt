Regional jet
A regional jet (RJ) is a jet airliner and a regional airliner with fewer than 100 seats. The first one was the Sud-Aviation Caravelle in 1959, followed by the widespread Yakovlev Yak-40, Fokker F-28 and BAe 146. The 1990s saw the emergence of the most widespread Canadair Regional Jet and its Embraer Regional Jet counterpart, then the larger Embraer E-Jet and multiple competing projects. In the US, they are limited in size by scope clauses. While the market was fragmented in the between the 1990s and 2019, it was consolidated until Bombardier Aviation sold its airliner programs between 2017 and 2019 and Boeing bought Embraer airliners in 2019, mimicking the Airbus-Boeing duopoly in the large jet airliner market, while emerging players try to push competitors: the Mitsubishi Regional Jet, Sukhoi Superjet 100, Comac ARJ21 and Antonov An-148. Definition Regional Jet is an industry jargon and not a regulatory category. Embry–Riddle Aeronautical University defines the regional jet as up to 100-seats capacity. This is also the limit capacity for two flight attendants.

Corey Feldman
Corey Scott Feldman (born July 16, 1971) is an American actor, voice actor, singer, and activist. He became well known during the 1980s, with roles as a youth in films such as Gremlins (1984), The Goonies (1985), and Stand by Me (1986). In 1987, Feldman starred in the horror film The Lost Boys with Corey Haim; they became known as "The Two Coreys" and went on to appear in other films together, including License to Drive (1988) and Dream a Little Dream (1989). Early life Feldman was born in Reseda, California, the son of a musician, Bob Feldman, and a cocktail waitress, Sheila Feldman. Feldman was raised Jewish and also holds beliefs in the paranormal. Career Child actor Feldman started his career at the age of three, appearing in a McDonald's commercial. In his youth, he appeared in over 100 television commercials and on 50 television series, including The Bad News Bears, Mork & Mindy, Eight is Enough, One Day at a Time and Cheers.

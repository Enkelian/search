1966 Los Angeles Rams season
The 1966 Los Angeles Rams season was the team's 29th year with the National Football League and the 21st season in Los Angeles. Los Angeles finished in third place in the Western Conference, four games behind the Green Bay Packers. The Rams were led by first-year head coach George Allen, who was inducted in the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2002.

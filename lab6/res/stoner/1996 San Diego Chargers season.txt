1996 San Diego Chargers season
The 1996 San Diego Chargers season was the franchise’s 37th, its 27th in the National Football League (NFL), and its 34th in San Diego. The season began with the team trying to improve on their 9–7 record in 1995. It was Bobby Ross's final season as the team's head coach, as he would take the Detroit Lions' head coaching job the following year. The Chargers fell back by one game and missed the playoffs by that margin.

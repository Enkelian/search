1942 in film
The year 1942 in film involved some significant events, in particular the release of a film consistently rated as one of the greatest of all time, Casablanca. Top-grossing films (U.S.) Events January 16 – Actress Carole Lombard is killed in a plane crash west of Las Vegas while returning home to Los Angeles from a War Bond tour. June 4 – Mrs. Miniver, starring Greer Garson and Walter Pidgeon, opens at Radio City Music Hall in New York, in what will become a record-breaking 10-week run. August 8 – Walt Disney's animated film Bambi opens in United Kingdom. November 11 – Road to Morocco, starring Bob Hope, Bing Crosby, and Dorothy Lamour, premieres. November 26 – The film Casablanca premieres at the Hollywood Theatre in New York City.

Dudjom Jigdral Yeshe Dorje
Dudjom Jigdral Yeshe Dorje (Tibetan: བདུད་འཇོམས་འཇིགས་བྲལ་ཡེ་ཤེས་རྡོ་རྗེ།, Wylie: bdud 'joms 'jigs bral ye shes rdo rje, THL Düjom Jikdrel Yéshé Dorjé) (1904–17 January 1987), was the second Dudjom Rinpoche. He was recognized as a direct rebirth of Dudjom Lingpa (1835–1904) and was also later appointed the first supreme head of the Nyingma lineage of Tibetan Buddhism by the fourteenth Dalai Lama and the Central Tibetan Administration. Biography Introduction Dudjom Rinpoche was born in southern Tibet in a region called Pemakö (now Mêdog County), which is known in Tibetan as a beyul (Wylie: sbas yul) or "hidden land". The second Dudjom Rinpoche was known as Jigdral Yeshe Dorje; Jigdral (Wylie: 'jigs bral) "fearless" was the name given to him by Khakyab Dorje, the fifteenth Karmapa. He was named Jñāna as a child, a Sanskrit term of which "Yeshe" (Wylie: ye shes) is the usual Tibetan translation. His father was Kathok Tulku Norbu Tenzing, who was a famous tulku in the Pema Kö region who had been trained in the Katok Monastery. His mother was Namgyal Drolma, descended from Ratna Lingpa.

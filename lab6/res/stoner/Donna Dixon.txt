Donna Dixon
Donna Lynn Dixon (born July 20, 1957) is an American born actress, art collector, philanthropist, and explorer. Biography Dixon was born on July 20, 1957, in Alexandria, Virginia, to Earl Dixon. Her father owned a nightclub in Lorton, Virginia on U.S. 1 called Hillbilly Heaven.Dixon began her career as a model and was named Miss Virginia USA in 1976 and Miss District of Columbia World in 1977. She competed in both Miss USA 1976 and Miss World USA 1977. She co-starred with Tom Hanks in the early 1980s situation comedy Bosom Buddies, playing the role of Sonny Lumet. Months after they worked together in the film Doctor Detroit (1983), Dixon and actor Dan Aykroyd married. They later starred together in the films Twilight Zone: The Movie (1983) (though they did not share any scenes), Spies Like Us (1985) and The Couch Trip (1988). Dixon and Aykroyd have three daughters. Presently Dixon spends her time advocating for the causes close to her heart; most pressingly climate change and wildlife extinction.

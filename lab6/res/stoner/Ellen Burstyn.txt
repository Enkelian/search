Ellen Burstyn
Ellen Burstyn (born Edna Rae Gillooly; December 7, 1932) is an American actress. She is known for her work in television movies, and portrayal of troubled women in dramas. Burstyn is the recipient of various accolades, and is among the few performers to have won Academy, Emmy and Tony Awards. Born in Detroit, Michigan, Burstyn left school and worked as a dancer and model. She made her stage debut on Broadway in 1957 and soon started to make appearances in television shows. Stardom followed soon after with her acclaimed role in The Last Picture Show (1971), which earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. Her next appearance in The Exorcist (1973), earned her a second Best Actress nomination. The film has remained popular and several publications have regarded it as one of the greatest horror films of all time. She also starred in Martin Scorsese's Alice Doesn't Live Here Anymore (1974), which won her the Academy Award for Best Actress.

Santa Fe County
Santa Fe County (Spanish: Condado de Santa Fe; meaning Holy faith in Spanish) is a county in the U.S. state of New Mexico. As of the 2010 census, the population was 144,170, making it New Mexico's third-most populous county, after Bernalillo County and Do√±a Ana County. Its county seat is Santa Fe, the state capital. Santa Fe County includes the Santa Fe Metropolitan Statistical Area, which is also included in the Albuquerque-Santa Fe-Las Vegas, Combined Statistical Area. Geography According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 1,911 square miles (4,950 km2), of which 1,909 square miles (4,940 km2) is land and 1.5 square miles (3.9 km2) (0.08%) is water. It is the fifth-smallest county in New Mexico by area. The highest point in the county is the summit of Santa Fe Baldy at 3,847 meters (12,621 ft). It is drained by the Rio Grande and several of its small tributaries.

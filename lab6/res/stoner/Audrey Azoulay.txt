Audrey Azoulay
Audrey Azoulay (French pronunciation: ​[o.dʁɛ a.zu.lɛ]; born 4 August 1972) is a French civil servant and politician who served as France's Minister of Culture from 2016 to 2017. She was nominated on 13 October 2017 and, on 10 November 2017, appointed as the Director-General of the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO), becoming the second female leader of the organization. Early life and education Early life and family Azoulay was born in Paris to a Moroccan Jewish family from Essaouira. She is the daughter of André Azoulay,former adviser to the king of Morocco Hassan II from 1991,then the current adviser to king Mohammed VI of Morocco.She is the daughter of Katia Brami a moroccan writer. Her aunt, Eliane Azoulay, is a journalist for the French magazine "Télérama". She indicates having "grown up in a very left-environnment"," politicized on the israeli-Palestinian conflict", in the Beaugrenelle neighbourhood, with her two older sisters. Judith, who worked in the "Association Française d'Action Artistique" (AFAA) and Sabrina who is a producer. She has a son and a daughter with her husband François-Xavier Labarraque, who also studied in Ecole Nationale d'administration. She doesn't have Moroccan nationality, unlike her parents.

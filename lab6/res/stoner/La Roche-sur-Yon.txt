La Roche-sur-Yon
La Roche-sur-Yon (pronounced [la ʁɔʃ syʁ jɔ̃]) is a commune in the Vendée department in the Pays de la Loire region in western France. It is the capital of the department. The demonym for its inhabitants is Yonnais. History The town expanded significantly after Napoleon I chose the site as the new préfecture of the Vendée on 25 May 1804, replacing Fontenay-le-Comte (then under its revolutionary name of Fontenay-le-Peuple). At the time, most of La Roche had been eradicated in the Vendée Revolt (1793–96); the renamed Napoléonville was laid out and a fresh population of soldiers and civil servants was brought in. Napoléonville was designed to accommodate 15,000 people.The town was called successively: La Roche-sur-Yon (during the Ancien Régime and the French First Republic) Napoléon-sur-Yon (during the First French Empire) Bourbon-Vendée (during the French Restoration) Napoléon-Vendée (during the French Second Empire) Geography The river Yon flows southward through the commune and crosses the town.

1915 Brooklyn Robins season
The 1915 Brooklyn Robins improved enough to finish in third place, just 10 games behind the National League champion Philadelphia Phillies. Regular season Season standings Record vs. opponents Notable transactions April 23, 1915: Dick Egan was purchased from the Robins by the Boston Braves. June 13, 1915: Phil Douglas was purchased by the Robins from the Cincinnati Reds. August 1915: Joe Schultz and cash were traded by the Robins to the Chicago Cubs for Larry Cheney. September 8, 1915: Phil Douglas was purchased from the Robins by the Chicago Cubs.

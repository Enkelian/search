1978 in film
The year 1978 in film involved some significant events. March 1 – Charlie Chaplin's coffin is stolen from a Swiss cemetery three months after burial. After recovery a few weeks later, the casket is sealed in a concrete vault which is then buried in the same grave. March – Leigh Brackett completes the first draft for The Empire Strikes Back, but dies only two weeks later. June – Daniel Melnick becomes head of Columbia Pictures after the David Begelman scandal. June 4 – Grease, starring John Travolta and Olivia Newton-John, has its world premiere at Grauman's Chinese Theatre in Hollywood. July 20 - Alan Hirschfield is fired as president and CEO of Columbia Pictures. He is replaced by Francis T. Vincent. August – Production begins on Star Trek: The Motion Picture.

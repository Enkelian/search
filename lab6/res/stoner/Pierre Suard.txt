Pierre Suard
Pierre Suard, born November 9, 1934 in Lons-le-Saunier, France, is an engineer, French senior official, and director of national companies. He is an alumnus of École Polytechnique and École des Ponts ParisTech (ENPC). Career After studying at École Polytechnique and ENPC (Ponts-et-Chaussées), Suard began working at the Compagnie Générale d’Électricité (CGE) in 1973. He was named CEO of the subsidiary Les Câbles de Lyon (Lyon Cable), which moved from fifth place to first in international ranking during the three years of his tenure. In 1986, when CGE was privatized, he was named CEO of the group by prime minister Édouard Balladur. In 1991, CGE became Alcatel Alsthom, and in 1998 the company’s name was changed to Alcatel. Suard presided over the development of the first digital telephone exchanges, as well as that of fiber optic cables, which were eventually bought by ITT. He transformed a French company that was known foremost for its close relationship with the government into an international industry with a global strategy.

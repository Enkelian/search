Brit Air
Brit Air (short for Brittany Air International) was a regional airline based at Morlaix Airport in Ploujean, Morlaix, Brittany, France, operating scheduled services as an Air France franchise from Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport, Paris-Orly Airport and Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport.The airline, along with Régional and Airlinair, was fully merged in HOP! since 2017 after a year of negotiation process. History The airline was established in 1973 and started operations in 1975. It was founded to provide services for business executives from western France, and introduced regular flights to London Gatwick in 1979. On 1 December 1995, Brit Air signed a franchising agreement with Air France Europe. It became a wholly owned subsidiary of Air France in October 2000. It employs 1,260 staff.Since 31 March 2013, all Brit Air flights are operated under HOP! name, Air France's new regional brand name.Brit Air ceased all flight operations in March 2017 after its merger in HOP!.

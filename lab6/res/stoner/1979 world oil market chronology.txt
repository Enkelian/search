1979 world oil market chronology
January: First emergency Crude Oil Buy-Sell Program allocations. January 16: Shah leaves Iran on vacation to Egypt . Bakhtiar government established by the Shah to preside until unrest subsides. January 20: Saudi Arabia announces drastic cut in first-quarter production. 9.5 MMBD ceiling imposed. Although actual cuts never reach announced levels, spot prices of Middle East light crudes rise 36 percent. January 20: One million Iranians march in Tehran in a show of support for the exiled Ayatollah Khomeini, Muslim leader. February 12: Bakhtiar resigns as prime minister of Iran after losing support of the military. March 5: Iran resumes petroleum exports. Spring: Gasoline shortage/world oil glut. March 26: OPEC makes full 14.5 percent price increase for 1979 effective on April 1. Marker crude raised to $14.56 per barrel. May: United States Department of Energy (DOE) announces $5 per barrel entitlement to importers of heating oil. Saudi Arabia announces intention to increase direct sales and to sell less through Aramco. Both announcements send prices higher. June 1: Phased oil price decontrol begins.

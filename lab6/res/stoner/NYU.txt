NYU
New York University (NYU) is a private research university based in New York City. NYU is ranked among the top 30 undergraduate colleges in the United States, and is highly selective, admitting only 15% of more than 85,000 applicants to the class of 2024. It is designated by Kaplan as one of the "New Ivies".Founded in 1831 as an institution to "admit [students] based upon merit rather than birthright or social class", NYU's historical campus is in Greenwich Village, Lower Manhattan. NYU is the largest independent research university in the United States, but also has degree-granting campuses in Abu Dhabi and Shanghai, and academic centers in Accra, Berlin, Buenos Aires, Florence, London, Los Angeles, Madrid, Paris, Prague, Sydney, Tel Aviv, and Washington, D.C.NYU is organized into 25 schools, including 10 undergraduate school and numerous graduate schools. The university has numerous research efforts, including research partnerships with the Inception Institute of Artificial Intelligence and with major technology firms like IBM.

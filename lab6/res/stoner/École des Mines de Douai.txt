École des Mines de Douai
The IMT Lille Douai or "École nationale supérieure des Mines-Télécom de Lille-Douai" (previously École des Mines de Douai or Mines Douai) is a public French National Graduate School of Engineering (Grande école d'ingénieurs). It is located in the Hauts-de-France region, shared between 2 campuses: the science campus ("Cité Scientifique") of the University of Lille (Villeneuve-d'Ascq, European Metropolis of Lille); and the city of Douai. It is accredited by the Commission des Titres d'Ingénieur (CTI) to deliver the French Diplôme d'Ingénieur. The school trains high-level engineers and scientists (Master and PhD level) in various technological fields. A small number of students are accepted each year, after a very selective exam. It is a crucial part of the infrastructure of French industry. It is affiliated to University of Lille and Institut Mines-Télécom (Mines Télécom Institut of Technology - IMT). The École des Mines de Douai merges with TELECOM Lille in 2017; it becomes the largest engineering school in France north of Paris under the name IMT Lille Douai. History The École des Maîtres-ouvriers mineurs de Douai was founded on 27 March 1878 on the same model as the École des mines d'Alès, founded forty years earlier.

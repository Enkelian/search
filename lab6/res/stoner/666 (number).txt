666 (number)
666 (six hundred [and] sixty-six) is the natural number following 665 and preceding 667. 666 is called the "number of the Beast" in (most manuscripts of) chapter 13 of the Book of Revelation, of the New Testament, and also in popular culture. In mathematics 666 is the sum of the first 36 natural numbers ( ∑ i = 1 36 i {\displaystyle \sum _{i=1}^{36}i} , i.e. 1 + 2 + 3 + ... + 34 + 35 + 36 = 666), and thus it is a triangular number. Notice that 36 = 15 + 21; 15, 21, and 36 are also triangular numbers; and 152 + 212 = 225 + 441 = 666. In base 10, 666 is a repdigit (and therefore a palindromic number) and a Smith number. A prime reciprocal magic square based on 1/149 in base 10 has a magic total of 666. The prime factorization of 666 is 2 • 32 • 37.

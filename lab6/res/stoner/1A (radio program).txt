1A (radio program)
1A is an American radio talk show produced by WAMU in Washington, D.C. and distributed nationally by NPR. The show debuted on January 2, 2017, airing on more than 340 NPR member stations in 35 states, Washington, D.C., and the U.S. Virgin Islands. It is also heard on Sirius XM on channel 122 several times each weekday. Journalist Joshua Johnson served as the program's host from 2017 to 2019, before leaving to work for MSNBC. Todd Zwillich replaced him as interim host but he announced on 30 March 2020 that he was moving to Vice, before 1A had its permanent replacement to Johnson. The current substitute host is Sasha-Ann Simons who, since 2017, has been "reporting on issues of race, identity, and economic mobility for WAMU", the producing host-station for 1A. Format The show airs live Monday through Friday from 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 noon ET (for many years, the time slot of WAMU's The Diane Rehm Show), but some local stations air it at a later time.

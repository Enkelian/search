Bushido (rapper)
Anis Mohamed Youssef Ferchichi (Arabic: أنيس محمد يوسف الفرشيشي‎; born 28 September 1978 in Bonn, North Rhine-Westphalia), better known by his aliases Bushido and Sonny Black, is a German rapper, hip-hop producer, entrepreneur and co-founder of record label ersguterjunge of Tunisian descent. Born in Bonn and raised by a single mother in Berlin, Tempelhof, Ferchichi came in contact with hip-hop through graffiti and his friend and fellow rapper Fler. Ferchichi released his demo, King of KingZ in 2001, which later led him to sign a record label deal with Aggro Berlin. Ferchichi's first official release was Carlo Cokxxx Nutten, released in October 2002, a collobaration with Fler. His first solo release, Vom Bordstein bis zur Skyline (2003) peaked at number 88 in Germany. Life and career 1978–98: Early life Ferchichi was born to a Tunisian father and a German mother in Bad Godesberg, Bonn, West Germany in September 1978. The father left the family when he was three years old and his mother decided to move to West-Berlin. He grew up with his brother in Berlin-Tempelhof, where he attended two gymnasiums before dropping out in eleventh grade.

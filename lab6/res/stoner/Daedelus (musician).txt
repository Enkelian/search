Daedelus (musician)
Alfred Darlington (born Alfred Weisberg-Roberts, October 31, 1977), better known by his stage name Daedelus, is an American record producer based in Los Angeles, California. He is a part of the groups The Long Lost and Adventure Time. He is affiliated with the internet radio station Dublab. Early life He attended the University of Southern California Thornton School of Music studying Jazz on Double Bass. Career A teenage obsession with Greek mythology led him to adopt the stage name Daedelus—a tribute to the artist, inventor, and craftsman (spelled Daedalus).In 1999 he began DJing Dublab.com's Entropy Sessions and releasing his own early demo productions. This got the attention of Carlos Niño (of Ammoncontact), who featured Daedelus tracks on two compilations. Subsequently, Daedelus released a studio album, Invention, on Plug Research in 2002. The Household EP was released on Eastern Developments in 2003. He also released The Weather, a collaborative album with Busdriver and Radioinactive, on Mush Records that year.He released a studio album, Of Snowdonia, on Plug Research in 2004.

Bath Spa railway station
Bath Spa railway station is the principal station serving the city of Bath in South West England. It is on the Great Western Main Line, 106 miles 71 chains (172.0 km) down the line from London Paddington between Chippenham to the east and Oldfield Park to the west. Its three-letter station code is BTH. The station is managed by Great Western Railway, and is served by trains operated by CrossCountry, Great Western Railway and South Western Railway. Architecture Bath Spa station was built in 1840 for the Great Western Railway by Brunel and is a Grade II* listed building. It is in an asymmetrical Tudor style with curving gables on the north bank of the Avon where the line curves across from the southern bank to the station and then back again. Opened on 31 August 1840, the station was named Bath and was renamed Bath Spa in 1949 to distinguish it from Bath Green Park station, which did not have its name altered from Bath until 1951.A convenient feature for passengers was ramps that led up to both platforms, giving the disabled and those with luggage easy access from the platforms to cars or taxis.

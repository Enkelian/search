Pablo Coira
Pablo Coira Lojo (born 18 October 1979 in Vilagarcía de Arousa, Pontevedra, Galicia) is a Spanish retired footballer who played mainly as a right back. Football career After starting playing professional football with local SD Compostela, in Segunda División, Coira joined Galicia giants Celta de Vigo in 1999, appearing sparingly throughout four La Liga seasons (maximum 18 league games in 2001–02). Shortly before joining Celta he represented Spain at the 1999 FIFA World Youth Championship, winning the tournament; also in the squad was teammate Pablo Couñago, who was crowned the competition's top scorer.Subsequently, Coira had unassuming stints with Deportivo Alavés – playing no matches in his final year, which ended in top flight relegation, and being loaned to Recreativo de Huelva in between – and Greek club Aris Thessaloniki FC. He returned to Spain in January 2007 to play for lowly UE Figueres in Segunda División B, named UE Casteldefells shortly after. In the 2008–09 campaign, Coira dropped down to Tercera División and joined RCD Espanyol's B-team.

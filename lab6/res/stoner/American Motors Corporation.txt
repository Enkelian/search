American Motors Corporation
American Motors Corporation (AMC; sometimes referred to as American Motors) was primarily an American automobile manufacturing company formed by the merger of Nash-Kelvinator Corporation and Hudson Motor Car Company on 1 May 1954. At the time, it was the largest corporate merger in U.S. history.AMC's main competitors were those automakers who held similar annual sales such as Studebaker, Packard, Kaiser and Willys-Overland.

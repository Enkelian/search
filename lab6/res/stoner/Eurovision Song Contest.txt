Eurovision Song Contest
The Eurovision Song Contest (French: Concours Eurovision de la chanson) is an annual international song competition, held every year by the Eurovision broadcasting organisation since 1956 (with the exception of 2020), with participants representing primarily European countries. Each participating country submits an original song to be performed on live television and radio, then casts votes for the other countries' songs to determine the winner. At least 50 countries are eligible to compete as of 2019; since 2015, Australia has been allowed as a contestant.Based on the Sanremo Music Festival held in Italy since 1951, Eurovision had been broadcasting every year from its inauguration in 1956 until 2019, due to the cancellation of the 2020 edition, making it the longest-running annual international television contest and one of the world's longest-running television programmes. The programme had been held every year for 64 years before the COVID-19 pandemic forced the EBU to cancel the 2020 contest. It is also one of the most watched non-sporting events, with audience figures of between 100 million and 600 million internationally.

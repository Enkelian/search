Mike Moran (music producer)
Michael "Mike" Moran (born 4 March 1948 Leeds, West Yorkshire, England) is a musician, songwriter and record producer. Biography Moran studied at the Royal College of Music in London prior to becoming a session musician and composer and arranger, including scoring music for such Handmade Films productions as Time Bandits (1981), The Missionary (1982) and Water (1985). He also played with the Ian Gillan Band. "Rock Bottom", which he wrote in partnership with Lynsey de Paul, was the UK entry in the Eurovision Song Contest 1977, and put him in the spotlight for the first time. Although leading early on in the voting, the song eventually came second in the contest. Nevertheless, it went on to become a Top 20 hit in many European countries including France, Germany, Austria and Switzerland, where it reached the top of their singles chart. It was more successful in Continental Europe than in the United Kingdom.

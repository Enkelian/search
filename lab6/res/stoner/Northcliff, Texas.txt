Northcliff, Texas
Northcliff is a former census-designated place (CDP) in Guadalupe County, Texas, United States. The population was 1,819 at the 2000 census. The area is now part of the city of Schertz and did not have its population recorded separately at the 2010 census. It is part of the San Antonio Metropolitan Statistical Area. Geography Northcliff is located in western Guadalupe County at 29°37′13″N 98°13′34″W (29.620267, -98.225986), just south of the Comal County line and east of Farm-to-Market Road 1103. According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP had a total area of 1.7 square miles (4.4 km2), all of it land. Demographics As of the census of 2000, there were 1,819 people, 719 households, and 552 families residing in the CDP. The population density was 1,088.6 people per square mile (420.6/km²). There were 743 housing units at an average density of 444.7/sq mi (171.8/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 82.30% White, 6.27% African American, 0.55% Native American, 1.92% Asian, 6.49% from other races, and 2.47% from two or more races.

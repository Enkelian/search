Pyrenees
The Pyrenees (; Spanish: Pirineos [piɾiˈneos]; French: Pyrénées [piʁene]; Aragonese: Pirineus; Catalan: Pirineus [piɾiˈnɛws]; Occitan: Pirenèus; Basque: Pirinioak [piɾini.o.ak]) is a range of mountains in southwest Europe that forms a natural border between Spain and France. Reaching a height of 3,404 metres (11,168 ft) altitude at the peak of Aneto, the range separates the Iberian Peninsula from the rest of continental Europe, and extends for about 491 km (305 mi) from the Bay of Biscay (Cap Higuer) to the Mediterranean Sea (Cap de Creus). The westernmost and easternmost ends of the range are low mountains straddled by two national minorities, the Basques and the Catalans, living on both sides of the border between Spain and France. For the most part -and with the notable exceptions of Occitan-speaking Val d'Aran, on the northern side of the watershed but administratively part of Spanish Catalonia, and of the Upper Cerdanya (Cerdagne), on the southern side of the watershed, but administratively part of French Catalonia- the main crest forms a divide between Spain and France, with the Catalan-speaking microstate of Andorra sandwiched in between.

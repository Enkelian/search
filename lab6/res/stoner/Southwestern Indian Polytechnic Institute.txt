Southwestern Indian Polytechnic Institute
Southwestern Indian Polytechnic Institute (SIPI) is a public community college in Albuquerque, New Mexico. It is federally operated by the Bureau of Indian Affairs and funded through the Bureau of Indian Education, an agency within the U. S. Department of the Interior. More than 120 different Indian Tribes are represented in SIPI's student body. History The Southwestern Indian Polytechnic Institute was conceived by the All Indian Pueblo Council, who envisioned a post-secondary school that could serve the Native American Community. Collective efforts with tribal leaders, public officials, and interested citizens, resulted in the school's founding in 1971; dedication ceremonies were held on August 21, 1971, and September 16, 1971, was the first day of classes. It operated initially on an "open-entry, open-exit system" of individualized training. In 1974, SIPI was awarded a citation for Excellence of Service. By 1975, SIPI was accredited at the Certificate Level by the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.In 1994, SIPI was designated a land grant institution, making it eligible for certain federal programs and part of a larger consortium of institutions.

Institutes of National Importance
Institute of National Importance (INI) is a status that may be conferred on a premier public higher education institution in India by an act of Parliament of India, an institution which "serves as a pivotal player in developing highly skilled personnel within the specified region of the country/state". Institutes of National Importance receive special recognition and funding from the Government of India. Acts Academy of Scientific and Innovative Research was declared as Institute of National Importance through Academy of Scientific and Innovative Research Act, 2010. All India Institutes of Medical Sciences are declared as Institutes of National Importance through the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (Amendment) Act, 2012. Indian Institutes of Technology are declared as Institutes of National Importance through the Institutes of Technology Act, 1961. National Institutes of Technology are declared as Institutes of National Importance through the National Institutes of Technology Act, 2007. Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Petroleum Technology was declared as Institute of National Importance through Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Petroleum Technology Act, 2007.

Ossetia
Ossetia (; Ossetian: Ир, Ирыстон, romanized: Ir, Iryston; Russian: Осетия, romanized: Osetiya; Georgian: ოსეთი, translit. Oseti) is an ethnolinguistic region located on both sides of the Greater Caucasus Mountains, largely inhabited by the Ossetians. The Ossetian language is part of the Eastern Iranian branch of the Indo-European languages family. The Ossetian-speaking area south of the main Caucasus ridge is recognized by most countries as within the borders of Georgia, but under the control of the Russian-backed de facto government of the Republic of South Ossetia. The northern portion of the region consists of the republic of North Ossetia–Alania within the Russian Federation. Recent history For earlier history, see Alans, Sarmatians 1774 — Ossetia becomes part of the Russian Empire 1922 — Creation of the South Ossetia and transformation of Ossetia into North Ossetia. North Ossetia remains a part of Russian SFSR, South Ossetia remains a part of Georgian SSR. 20 September 1990 — Independent Republic of South Ossetia. The republic remained unrecognized, yet it detached itself from Georgia de facto.

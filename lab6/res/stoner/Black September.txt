Black September
The Black September (Arabic: أيلول الأسود‎; Aylūl Al-Aswad) was a conflict fought in the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan between the Jordanian Armed Forces (JAF), under the leadership of King Hussein, and the Palestine Liberation Organisation (PLO), under the leadership of Yasser Arafat, primarily between 16 and 27 September 1970, with certain aspects of the conflict continuing until 17 July 1971. After Jordan lost control of the West Bank to Israel in 1967, Palestinian fighters known as fedayeen moved their bases to Jordan and stepped up their attacks on Israel and Israeli-occupied territories. Israeli retaliation on a PLO camp based in Karameh, a Jordanian town along the border with the West Bank, developed into a full-scale battle. During the 1968 Battle of Karameh, Palestinians and Jordanians fought against Israel, leading to a partial Arab victory and despite the Palestinians having limited success in inflicting Israeli casualties, King Hussein allowed them to take major credit, and as a result the support for Palestinian fedayeen within Jordan and the rest of the Arab world witnessed an uprising.

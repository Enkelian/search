1927 Brooklyn Robins season
The 1927 Brooklyn Robins had another bad year. They tied a National League record on May 21 by using five pitchers in the eighth inning. Offseason January 9, 1927: Burleigh Grimes was traded by the Robins to the New York Giants as part of a three-team trade. The Philadelphia Phillies sent Butch Henline to the Robins and George Harper to the Giants. The Giants sent Jack Scott and Fresco Thompson to the Phillies. March 29, 1927: Mickey O'Neil was purchased from the Robins by the Washington Senators. Regular season Season standings Record vs. opponents Notable transactions May 3, 1927: Bob McGraw was traded by the Robins to the St. Louis Cardinals for Jake Flowers.

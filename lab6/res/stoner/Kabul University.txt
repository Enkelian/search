Kabul University
Kabul University (KU) (Pashto: د کابل پوهنتون‎, romanized: Da Kābəl Pohantūn, Dari: پوهنتون کابل‎, romanized: Pohantūn-e-Kābul) is one of the major and oldest institutions of higher education in Afghanistan. It is located in the 3rd District of the capital Kabul, near the Ministry of Higher Education. It was founded in 1931 during the government of Mohammed Nadir Shah and then Prime Minister Mohammad Hashim Khan. Kabul University is attended by approximately 22,000 students. Of these, nearly 43% are female students. The mission of Kabul University is to mature and prosper as an internationally recognised institution of learning and research, a community of stakeholders committed to shared governance, and a centre of innovative thought and practice.

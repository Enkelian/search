1980 Los Angeles Dodgers season
The 1980 Los Angeles Dodgers finished the season in second place in the Western Division of the National League, one game behind the Houston Astros. Don Sutton set a Dodger record with his 52nd career shutout this season and the Dodgers also hosted the All-Star game for the first time. Offseason November 17, 1979: Don Stanhouse was signed as a free agent by the Dodgers. December 3, 1979: Von Joshua was selected off waivers from the Dodgers by the San Diego Padres. March 27, 1980: Johnny Oates was released by the Dodgers.

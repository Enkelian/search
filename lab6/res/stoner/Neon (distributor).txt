Neon (distributor)
Neon (stylized as NEON) is an American film production and distribution company founded by Tom Quinn and Tim League in 2017. The company is best known for releasing films, such as Ingrid Goes West, Beach Rats, I, Tonya, and Parasite. In September 2017, the company partnered with Blumhouse Productions to manage BH Tilt. As of 2019, League is no longer involved with daily operations for the company. Process During the 4th Annual Zurich Summit, Tom Quinn commented on Neon's intent to release titles that appeal to audiences who "skew under 45, that have no aversion to violence, no aversion to foreign language and to non-fiction." Accolades As of February 2020, Neon has received a total of 12 Academy Award nominations. In 2018, I, Tonya received three nominations, winning Best Supporting Actress for Allison Janney. In 2019, Border was nominated for Best Makeup and Hairstyling. In 2020, Neon experienced its most successful Oscar season yet with Parasite and Honeyland accruing eight nominations in total, with the former winning four awards including Best Picture, becoming the first non-English language film to receive that honor.

Canal (anatomy)
In anatomy, a canal (or canalis in Latin) is a tubular passage or channel which connects different regions of the body. Examples include: Cranial Region Alveolar canals Greater palatine canal Incisive canals Infraorbital canal Mandibular canal Palatovaginal canal Pterygoid canal Semicircular canal Lower Extremities Adductor canal Femoral canal Obturator canal Pelvic Region Anal canal Inguinal canal Pudendal canal See also Foramen Canaliculus References Dorland's Illustrated Medical Dictionary, 27th ed. 1988 W.B. Saunders Company. Philadelphia, PA.

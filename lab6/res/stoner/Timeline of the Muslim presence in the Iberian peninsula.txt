Timeline of the Muslim presence in the Iberian peninsula
This is a timeline of notable events during the period of Muslim presence in Iberia, starting with the Umayyad conquest in the 8th century. Conquest (711–756) 711 – A Berber force of about 7,000 soldiers under the Berber general Tariq ibn Ziyad, loyal to the Umayyad Emir of Damascus, Al-Walid I, enters the Iberian peninsula from North Africa. At the Battle of Guadalete Tariq ibn Ziyad defeats King Roderic. 712 – The Muslim governor of Northern Africa, Musa ibn Nusayr, follows Tariq ibn Ziyad with an army of 18,000 Arabs. He takes Medina-Sidonia, Seville and Mértola. 713 – Abd al-Aziz ibn Musa, Musa ibn Nusair's son, takes Jaén, Murcia, Granada, Sagunto. 714 – First Umayyad campaigns in the lower Ebro valley and southeast part of the Iberian Peninsula. Abd al-Aziz ibn Musa takes Évora, Santarém and Coimbra. 715 – Abd al-Aziz ibn Musa is left in charge and makes his capital the city of Seville.

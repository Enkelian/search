Zoltán Tóth (footballer, born 1955)
Zoltán Tóth (born 29 December 1955 in Budapest) is a Hungarian former footballer who played as a goalkeeper and currently works as a coach. He played for Újpest FC in Budapest from 1975 to 1979 and once played for the Hungary national team in 1979. Life in Hungary Tóth was born in Hungary to György Tóth, who played fifteen times with the Hungary national football team between 1939 and 1948. Tóth's talent was apparent early and he eventually also saw time with the Hungary himself. On a tour with the team in Cadiz, Spain in 1979, Tóth defected and moved to the United States.[1] He also played on the Hungarian Olympic team. Professional In addition to his national team time, Tóth spent four seasons with Hungarian First Division club Újpest FC. As one of the top Hungarian clubs, Újpest regularly played in the UEFA Cup and European Cup. They twice won the Hungarian national championship during Tóth's time, 1977–78 and 1978–79. In 1979, Tóth signed with the New York Arrows of Major Indoor Soccer League (MISL) for the 1980–81 season and got his start when Arrows' goalkeeper Shep Messing was injured. He was the 1982-1983 MISL Goalkeeper of the Year.
